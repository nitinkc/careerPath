*[IC]: Individual Contributor — engineer who contributes code/technical leadership without managing people
*[CTO]: Chief Technology Officer — executive responsible for company's technology vision and strategy
*[VP]: Vice President
*[OKR]: Objectives and Key Results — goal-setting framework
*[KPI]: Key Performance Indicator — measurable business metric
*[FAANG]: Facebook (Meta), Apple, Amazon, Netflix, Google — large tech companies
*[API]: Application Programming Interface
*[DevOps]: Development and Operations; culture of automation and infrastructure
*[CI/CD]: Continuous Integration / Continuous Deployment
*[System Design]: Architecture for building large-scale systems
*[DDIA]: Designing Data-Intensive Applications — foundational book on distributed systems
*[Microservices]: Architecture pattern using small, independent services
*[Monolith]: Single, unified codebase and deployment unit
*[Scalability]: Ability of system to handle increased load
*[Latency]: Time it takes for a request to be processed
*[Tech Debt]: Code/design issues that accumulate and slow future development
*[Code Review]: Process of peer evaluation before code merges
*[Mentoring]: Guiding junior engineers through problems and growth
*[Promotion]: Movement to next career level with increased responsibility
*[Board]: Company's board of directors; CTO may report to board
*[Stakeholder]: Anyone affected by or interested in project outcomes
*[Roadmap]: Plan of engineering initiatives for next quarter/year
*[Retrospective]: Team meeting to discuss what went well/poorly
*[On-call]: Responsibility to respond to production issues during scheduled time
*[SLA]: Service Level Agreement — commitment to availability/performance
*[MTTR]: Mean Time To Repair — average time to fix production issue
*[Incident]: Production outage or significant problem
*[Postmortem]: Analysis of incident to find root cause and prevent recurrence
*[Hiring]: Process of recruiting and onboarding new engineers
*[Retention]: Keeping engineers on team through good compensation and culture
*[Equity]: Stock options; significant part of tech compensation at startups
*[Vesting]: Time-based earning of company stock — typically 4 years
*[Sabbatical]: Extended break from work for learning or recovery
*[Staff Engineer]: Senior IC role with org-wide technical influence
*[Principal Engineer]: Highest IC rank; sets technology strategy across company
*[Director]: Management role overseeing 1–3 engineering managers
*[Engineering Manager]: First management role; manages 5–12 engineers
*[Tech Lead]: Combination IC and manager; hybrid role
*[Burnout]: Exhaustion from sustained overwork or misalignment
*[Career Ladder]: Defined progression of roles and compensation at company
*[Leveling]: Process of determining what level engineer operates at
*[Calibration]: Comparing engineer performance/level across org
*[Performance Review]: Annual or biannual discussion of engineer's work and goals
*[360 Review]: Anonymous feedback from peers, managers, reports
*[Career Development]: Plan for engineer's growth and progression
*[Skills Gap]: Area where engineer needs development
*[Competency]: Demonstrated ability in a specific skill area
*[Leadership]: Ability to influence and guide others toward goals
*[Influence]: Power to affect decisions without formal authority
*[Culture]: Shared values, norms, and practices of organization
*[Process]: Defined way of working; can help coordination or slow things down
*[Agile]: Software development framework emphasizing iteration and feedback
*[Waterfall]: Linear software development approach with distinct phases
*[Startup]: Young company with high growth potential but uncertain future
*[Scale-up]: Growing company that has found product-market fit
*[Enterprise]: Large, established company with stable revenue
*[Series A/B/C]: Funding rounds for startups; different company stages
*[IPO]: Initial Public Offering; company goes public
*[M&A]: Mergers and Acquisitions; company acquisition or merger
*[Exit]: When founders/early employees cash out (IPO or acquisition)
*[Market Share]: Percentage of total market served by company
*[Competitive Advantage]: Unique strength that differentiates company
*[Business Model]: How company makes money
*[Revenue]: Total income from customers
*[Profitability]: Revenue minus expenses
*[ARR]: Annual Recurring Revenue — predictable annual income
*[CAC]: Customer Acquisition Cost
*[LTV]: Lifetime Value of a customer
*[Burn Rate]: How fast company spends cash
*[Runway]: Months of cash remaining at current burn rate
*[Due Diligence]: Evaluation of company before acquisition or investment
*[Refactoring]: Restructuring code without changing external behavior
*[Legacy Code]: Old code that's hard to understand or change
*[Code Quality]: Measure of how well code is written
*[Testing]: Verification that code works as intended
*[Unit Test]: Test of single function or component
*[Integration Test]: Test of multiple components working together
*[E2E Test]: End-to-end test simulating real user workflow
*[Documentation]: Written explanation of code and systems
*[Onboarding]: Process of getting new engineer productive
*[Ramp Time]: Time until new engineer is fully productive
*[Tribal Knowledge]: Important information only known by few people
*[Knowledge Transfer]: Sharing information from one person to others
